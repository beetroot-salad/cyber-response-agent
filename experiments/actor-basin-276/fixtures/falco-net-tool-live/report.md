The Falco "Launch Suspicious Network Tool in Container" alert fired on container 7e76d1cea7c4 (`scanner-1`, image `soc-playground/host-plain:24.04`) running service account `svc.monitoring` (uid 1001). Lead l-004 established a 9,189-event 7-day baseline of identical `nc -z -w1 <host> <port>` readiness probes with ~8-10 minute cadence, fleet-wide across 11 playground containers — this is not novel behavior; the Falco rule appears newly deployed against a long-standing monitoring pattern. Lead l-005 confirmed the container is named `scanner-1` with cron running as an active process, explaining the periodic invocation cadence, and resolved uid 1001 to `svc.monitoring` (non-login shell). Lead l-006 queried the ±15-minute Falco window and found exactly 90 events, all from the single "Launch Suspicious Network Tool in Container" rule covering 8 hosts × 5 ports — zero co-occurring write-below-etc, read-sensitive-file-untrusted, write-below-tmp, or exfiltration events. Lead l-007 confirmed `svc.monitoring` is a provisioned service-account in the identity registry, explicitly authorized on scanner-1 via override entry and IAM-authorized on all 11 hosts in the scan scope including jump-box-1; no sudo, no interactive shell. The nc binary carrying `EXE_LOWER_LAYER` was present in the base image at build time, not installed post-boot. Recommended action: tune or allowlist the Falco rule for the `scanner-1` container image and `svc.monitoring` account to suppress future false-positive fires on this known-good health-check automation.
