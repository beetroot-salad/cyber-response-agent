**Lead-selection rationale.** The alert's summary statistics (38 distinct images, avg entropy 3.12, avg name length 14) are aggregate — they can't distinguish a mailmerge-spawned Acrobat+printdriver fleet from a macro dropper launching 38 renamed payloads. The actual child image paths and signing status are the cheapest single discriminator: Office legitimacy hypotheses all produce children that are recognizable signed binaries in expected install paths; every macro-dropper variant produces at least one image that falls outside that set. `process-lineage` on pid-9244 returns exactly that breakdown.

**Reading geometry.** lp1/lp2 partition on whether *all* images are clean vs *any* image is anomalous (a single unsigned or misplaced binary is sufficient to route escalation). lp3 covers the data-gap path — an empty or missing result forecloses lp1 and lp2 both, and the safe default is escalation.
