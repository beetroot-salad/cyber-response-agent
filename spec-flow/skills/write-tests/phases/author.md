## Charge — the author

Read **references/schema.md** for address forms. Consensus assertions are already written, so this is assembly and hardening, not authorship from scratch: reconcile the answered copies into one test per premise, apply the human's fork resolutions exactly (a resolution applied loosely is a fork re-opened silently), and author the situations only the strong author raised. Work with the ledger's refuted claims in hand — you **may not assert content a refutation marks wrong or stale**: pin the demanded correction (red against HEAD is the expected state of a spec), never the ambient string the handoff tells the implementer to change — a test asserting today's refuted behavior is the pinned-green shape, a probed bug hardened into a contract by its own spec.

Survey the project's existing test machinery first and build on it — a harness that already fakes the dependencies is the starting point, so a new scenario is a few lines of data, not fresh plumbing; the profile's `tests.harness` names it. Then:

- **Induce faults down this hierarchy, never off it.** (1) *Real input through the real primitive, in the test itself* — write the undecodable bytes, create the colliding stem, delete the file: the test re-probes reality on every run, and the taxonomy assumption ceases to exist rather than being pinned once. (2) When the dependency is too expensive or nondeterministic to drive (an LLM, a network), *a fake whose fault content — the exception class, the malformed shape — cites the ledger claim that observed it on the real dependency.* (3) An author-imagined fault is banned: if no claim covers it, request the probe (red-flag it in your digest and stop on that test) rather than inventing: a guard and its test built from the same wrong prior are green together.
- **Declarative fault-injection fakes** — one fake per dependency, driven by a data fault-spec (`fail_on`, `raise_after`, `malformed`, `delay`). The fake injects faults **only** — it never classifies or decides policy. Fakes enter through the entry point's injection seams (a deps parameter, a constructor argument), never by monkey-patching an attribute out from under the target — the profile's `tests.idioms` records what CI enforces here. If the design gives a dependency no seam, the seam is part of the contract — pin it as a demand.
- **Fakes record what they receive.** A payload (`kind: shape`) demand asserts on the *captured inbound payload* against the facet's invariants — the obligation carries the assertion content (rules.md R1) — not on the fake's canned response. A fake that only returns answers leaves the entire outbound channel unpinned.
- **One test per executable demand**, named by the demand's `discharged_by`, driving the **real entry point** against the fakes; assert observable outcomes only — output contents, return value, raised errors, recorded seam calls. This holds for a **census or parity demand** too, and that is where it is usually dropped: enumerating a registry and asserting a structural property of each entry (`isinstance`, `hasattr`, a field is not None) feels like discharge, but a type the production default already satisfies makes the assertion unfailable — it certifies that the *field exists*, never that it is *wired*. If the demand is "every X is connected to Y", drive one X and observe Y's effect; the enumeration is how you pick the subjects, not what you assert about them. `check_binds`' unexercised-seam scan catches the sharpest form, not the whole class. The test's **docstring carries the demand's prose** — the observable outcome and any threaded concept (`salt=deps.salt`) — because that docstring is what `check_binds` scans in place of a demand `outcome`; thread only concepts the demand `binds`, or bind them.
- **Every `kind: negative` demand is paired with its positive control** — on the same address, under the complementary condition (schema.md) — proof the mechanism fired and the observation channel can see the difference. The control takes the shape of the negative: for a redaction, the same bytes *are* returned through the sanctioned path; for a denied action, the allowed variant succeeds; for an absent artifact, the substitute workflow demonstrably completes. And a negative binds **every surface the content could reach** — each of the actor's out-edges (outputs, traces, error messages), not just the obvious one; an unbound surface is exactly where the leak ships. A bare negative passes vacuously (`assert secret not in out` is also green when `out` is empty).

Digest inventory: `{tests: n, demands_discharged: n, probe_requests: n}` with `inputs` echoing the resolutions and dispositions counts — every consensus assertion and every resolution lands in a test or is red-flagged, never silently dropped.
